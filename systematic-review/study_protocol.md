# 1. Introduction

## 1.1 Background

This project is a systematic review designed to identify the optimal delivery of multimodal prehabilitation for adults undergoing major cancer surgery.

Multimodal prehabilitation, comprising of exercise and nutritional interventions with or without psychological support, is increasingly implemented to improve the physiological reserve of patients undergoing cancer surgery and enhance their postoperative recovery. However, substantial variation exists in programme design, including intervention timing, duration, exercise prescription, nutritional strategies, supervision, and methods of delivery. Consequently, there remains uncertainty regarding the characteristics of the most effective prehabilitation programme.

The review aims to synthesise evidence from randomised controlled trials (RCTs) evaluating multimodal prehabilitation programmes delivered before major cancer surgery. Eligible studies include adult patients undergoing surgical resection for cancer and interventions consisting of at least exercise and nutritional support.

The review is expected to identify evidence-based recommendations regarding the optimal timing, duration, composition, supervision, and delivery of multimodal prehabilitation programmes. The findings will help inform clinical practice, support the development of standardised prehabilitation pathways, and identify priorities for future research in perioperative cancer care.

## 1.2 Objectives

This systematic review aims to identify and synthesise the optimal type, duration, timing, and delivery mode of exercise and nutritional interventions within multimodal prehabilitation programmes for adults undergoing major cancer surgery, including associated safety and feasibility considerations.

The review question is structured according to the PICOS framework.

# 2. Methods

The completed systematic review will be reported in accordance with the **PRISMA 2020** guidelines (Page et al., 2021).

## 2.1 Eligibility Criteria

Eligibility criteria are defined using the PICOS framework.

| PICOS | Inclusion Criteria | Exclusion Criteria |
|-------|--------------------|--------------------|
| **Population (P)** | Adult patients (≥18 years) diagnosed with cancer (colorectal, oesophagogastric, gastric, lung, pancreatic, liver, urologic, gynaecologic, breast, or mixed cancers) undergoing surgical resection. | Paediatric populations; non-cancer patients; patients not undergoing surgical resection. |
| **Intervention (I)** | Multimodal prehabilitation delivered in the preoperative period, comprising at least two components including exercise (aerobic and/or resistance training) and nutritional support (dietary counselling and/or protein/oral nutritional supplementation). | Unimodal prehabilitation; multimodal prehabilitation lacking either an exercise or nutrition component; interventions delivered exclusively postoperatively. |
| **Comparator (C)** | Standard perioperative care, postoperative rehabilitation, enhanced recovery after surgery (ERAS) protocols alone, or usual care without a structured prehabilitation component. | Studies without a true comparator group. |
| **Outcomes (O)** | At least one of: functional capacity (e.g., six-minute walk test, cardiopulmonary exercise testing), postoperative complications, length of hospital stay, health-related quality of life, nutritional status/body composition, neoadjuvant chemotherapy adherence. | Studies not reporting any predefined outcome. |
| **Study Design (S)** | Randomised controlled trials (including cluster and quasi-RCTs); published 2016–2026; English language; human studies; full text available. | Non-randomised studies; publications before 2016; non-English language; animal studies; conference abstracts/registry records without full text. |

Electronic searches will be conducted in:

- PubMed
- Cochrane Library
- Embase

The search strategy combines controlled vocabulary (MeSH in PubMed and Emtree in Embase) with free-text keywords for prehabilitation, cancer, and surgery.

## 2.3 Study Records

### 2.3.1 Data Management

Search results from all databases will be exported into Rayyan for reference management and screening.
- Duplicate records will be removed using Rayyan's automated tools followed by manual verification.
- Full-text exclusion reasons will be documented for the PRISMA flow diagram.

### 2.3.2 Selection Process

Study selection, data extraction, and risk-of-bias assessment will be undertaken by one reviewer.

A second reviewer will check screening decisions, extracted data and risk-of-bias assessments

Any disagreements will be resolved through discussion.

Data will be extracted using a standardised form, and methodological quality will be assessed using the Cochrane Risk of Bias 2 (RoB 2) tool (Sterne et al., 2019).

Due to expected clinical heterogeneity, findings will be synthesised narratively in accordance with PRISMA 2020 guidance.

---

## 2.4 Data Items

The following information will be extracted:

- Study identification (author, publication year)
- Study design (RCT type, sample size, dropouts)
- Cancer type
- Intervention characteristics:
  - Exercise modality
  - Intensity
  - Frequency
  - Session duration
  - Supervision
  - Nutritional counselling
  - Energy/protein targets
  - Supplementation
  - Additional interventions
  - Programme duration
  - Timing relative to surgery/neoadjuvant therapy
- Comparator description
- Outcome data:
  - Functional capacity
  - Postoperative complications
  - Length of hospital stay
  - Health-related quality of life
  - Nutritional status/body composition
  - Neoadjuvant chemotherapy adherence
  - Safety and feasibility outcomes

---

## 2.5 Outcomes

Primary outcomes include:

- Functional capacity (6MWT/6MWD or cardiopulmonary exercise testing)
- Postoperative complications (CCI, Clavien–Dindo classification, or complication incidence)
- Length of hospital stay
- Health-related quality of life (patient-reported outcome measures)
- Nutritional status/body composition (screening tools, biochemical markers, anthropometry, body composition)
- Neoadjuvant chemotherapy completion/adherence

---

## 2.6 Risk of Bias in Individual Studies

Methodological quality will be assessed using the Cochrane Risk of Bias 2 (RoB 2) tool.

The following domains will be assessed:

1. Bias arising from the randomisation process
2. Bias due to deviations from intended interventions
3. Bias due to missing outcome data
4. Bias in measurement of the outcome
5. Bias in selection of the reported result

Each domain and the overall study will be rated as:

- Low risk
- Some concerns
- High risk

---

## 2.7 Data Synthesis

Extracted data will be organised according to:

- Study characteristics
- Cancer type
- Intervention components
- Programme duration
- Comparator
- Adherence
- Outcomes

Given anticipated heterogeneity across cancer types, intervention components, and outcome measures, a quantitative meta-analysis is not expected to be appropriate.

A structured narrative synthesis will therefore be conducted.
